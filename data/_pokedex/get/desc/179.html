Its fluffy coat swells to double when static electricity builds up. Touching it can be shocking. When cold weather increases static electricity, its wool doubles in size and the tip of its tail glows slightly. It stores lots of air in its soft fur, allowing it to stay cool in summer and warm in winter. Mareep’s fluffy coat of wool rubs together and builds a static charge. The more static electricity is charged, the more brightly the lightbulb at the tip of its tail glows. Clothing made from Mareep’s fleece is easily charged with static electricity, so a special process is used on it.
