It swims backward by opening and closing its two shells. Its large tongue is always kept hanging out. Its hard shell repels any kind of attack. It is vulnerable only when its shell is open. Clamping on to an opponent reveals its vulnerable parts, so it uses this move only as a last resort. At night, this Pokémon uses its broad tongue to burrow a hole in the seafloor sand and then sleep in it. While it is sleeping, Shellder closes its shell, but leaves its tongue hanging out. This Pokémon’s tongue is always hanging out. It uses its tongue with great dexterity to dig up sand from the seabed in its search for food. The sand that accumulates inside its shell eventually becomes a pearl. But the pearl gets in the way, so it spits it out and discards it.
